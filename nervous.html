Nervous System

Discover the complex communication network of the human body.

Overview

The nervous system is the body's primary control and communication network. It processes sensory information, controls motor functions, and is responsible for cognition, emotions, and memory. Learn more at Britannica – Nervous System.

Major Divisions

  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Includes the brain and spinal cord. It processes and responds to sensory input. [NCBI Article]
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Made up of nerves that branch from the CNS to the rest of the body.
    • Somatic Nervous System: Controls voluntary movements.
    • Autonomic Nervous System: Controls involuntary functions like heartbeat and digestion.
      • Sympathetic: "Fight or flight" response
      • Parasympathetic: "Rest and digest" response

Key Structures

  • Brain: The control center for thoughts, emotions, memory, and coordination. [Hopkins Medicine]
  • Spinal Cord: A communication highway between brain and body.
  • Nerves: Bundles of neurons that transmit signals across the body.
  • Neurons: Basic units of the nervous system that transmit electrical impulses. [NCBI - Neurons]

Functions

  • Receives and interprets sensory input
  • Controls voluntary and involuntary movements
  • Regulates bodily functions (e.g., heart rate, digestion)
  • Enables thought, memory, emotion, and language

How It Works

Neurons transmit electrical impulses across synapses using chemical messengers called neurotransmitters. This allows for rapid communication between the brain, spinal cord, and body. [Watch on Khan Academy]

Common Nervous System Disorders

  • Epilepsy: A condition involving recurrent seizures due to abnormal brain activity.
  • Parkinson’s Disease: A neurodegenerative disorder affecting movement.
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS): An autoimmune condition damaging nerve coverings (myelin).
  • Alzheimer’s Disease: A progressive disease causing memory loss and cognitive decline. [Alzheimer’s Association]

Fun Facts

  • Your brain has around 86 billion neurons.
  • Nerve impulses can travel up to 250 mph (402 km/h).
  • The spinal cord is about 18 inches (45 cm) long.
