Digestive System

Explore how the body processes food and absorbs nutrients.

Overview

The digestive system breaks down food into nutrients the body can use and eliminates waste. It includes a complex network of organs working together to process what we eat. Learn more at Britannica – Digestive System.

Main Organs and Their Roles

  • Mouth: Begins mechanical and chemical digestion through chewing and saliva.
  • Esophagus: A muscular tube that transports food to the stomach.
  • Stomach: Breaks down food with acid and enzymes. [Cleveland Clinic – Stomach]
  • Small Intestine: The site of most nutrient absorption. Divided into:
    • Duodenum
    • Jejunum
    • Ileum
  • Large Intestine (Colon): Absorbs water and forms waste (feces).
  • Rectum and Anus: Store and eliminate waste.

Accessory Organs

  • Liver: Produces bile for fat digestion and detoxifies blood. [NCBI – Liver Function]
  • Gallbladder: Stores bile and releases it during digestion.
  • Pancreas: Produces enzymes and hormones like insulin.

Digestion Process

  1. Ingestion: Eating food.
  2. Propulsion: Swallowing and peristalsis move food through the tract.
  3. Mechanical Breakdown: Chewing and churning in the stomach.
  4. Chemical Digestion: Enzymes and acids break down molecules.
  5. Absorption: Nutrients absorbed mainly in the small intestine.
  6. Defecation: Removal of undigested waste.

Common Digestive Disorders

  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD): Stomach acid flows back into the esophagus.
  • Ulcers: Sores in the stomach lining caused by acid or infection.
  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS): A chronic condition with abdominal discomfort and altered bowel habits. [NIDDK – IBS]
  • Lactose Intolerance: Inability to digest lactose, a sugar in milk.

Interesting Facts

  • The small intestine is over 6 meters long!
  • It takes about 24 to 72 hours to fully digest food.
  • The stomach produces a new layer of mucus every few hours to protect itself from acid.
