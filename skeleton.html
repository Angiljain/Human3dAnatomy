Human Skeletal System

Explore the framework that gives shape and protection to our body.

Overview

The human skeletal system is the internal framework that supports the body and protects vital organs. Composed of 206 bones in adults, it also includes cartilage, joints, and ligaments. This system is essential not only for physical structure but also for movement, blood cell production, and mineral storage.

Major Components

  • Skull: Protects the brain and forms the structure of the face.
  • Vertebral Column: Backbone made up of 33 vertebrae; protects the spinal cord.
  • Rib Cage: Protects the heart and lungs; includes 12 pairs of ribs.
  • Pelvic Girdle: Supports abdominal organs and connects the spine to the lower limbs.
  • Limbs: Includes bones of arms (humerus, radius, ulna) and legs (femur, tibia, fibula).
  • Joints: Points where two or more bones meet (e.g., knees, elbows).

Functions of the Skeletal System

  • Support: Provides the structural framework for the body.
  • Protection: Shields internal organs like the brain, heart, and lungs.
  • Movement: Works with muscles to enable body motion.
  • Mineral Storage: Stores essential minerals like calcium and phosphorus.
  • Blood Cell Production: Red bone marrow produces red and white blood cells.
  • Energy Storage: Yellow marrow stores fat as an energy reserve.

Interesting Facts

  • The femur is the longest and strongest bone in the body.
  • Babies are born with about 270 bones, but some fuse as they grow.
  • The smallest bone is the stapes in the ear.
