Muscular System

Explore how muscles power movement and stability in the human body.

Overview

The muscular system is responsible for body movement, posture maintenance, joint stability, and heat production. It includes over 600 muscles working under voluntary and involuntary control.

Main Muscle Types

  • Skeletal Muscles: Voluntary muscles attached to bones that move the body.
  • Cardiac Muscle: Involuntary muscle found only in the heart, responsible for pumping blood.
  • Smooth Muscles: Involuntary muscles in organs like the stomach, intestines, and blood vessels.

Major Muscle Groups

  • Head & Neck: Facial muscles, sternocleidomastoid
  • Torso: Pectorals, abdominals, obliques
  • Back: Trapezius, latissimus dorsi
  • Arms: Biceps, triceps, forearm muscles
  • Legs: Quadriceps, hamstrings, gluteals, calves

Functions

  • Enable movement through contraction and relaxation
  • Maintain posture and body position
  • Stabilize joints
  • Generate body heat to maintain temperature

How It Works

Muscles contract when stimulated by electrical impulses from the nervous system. Skeletal muscles pull on bones, cardiac muscle contracts rhythmically without rest, and smooth muscles work automatically in internal organs.

Common Muscular Disorders

  • Muscle strain: Overstretching or tearing of muscle fibers.
  • Muscular dystrophy: Genetic conditions causing progressive muscle weakness.
  • Myasthenia gravis: Autoimmune condition that weakens skeletal muscles.

Fun Facts

  • The strongest muscle (by weight) is the masseter (jaw muscle).
  • Muscles make up about 40% of total body weight.
  • The tongue is made of eight muscles working together.
