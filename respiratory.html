Respiratory System

Understand how we breathe and exchange gases with the environment.

Overview

The respiratory system is responsible for the exchange of gases—primarily oxygen and carbon dioxide—between the body and the environment. It ensures that oxygen is delivered to the blood and carbon dioxide is removed. Learn more at Britannica – Respiratory System.

Main Components

  • Nose/Nasal Cavity: Warms, moistens, and filters the air.
  • Pharynx and Larynx: Air passes through the throat and voice box.
  • Trachea: A windpipe that directs air to the lungs.
  • Bronchi & Bronchioles: Tubes that branch into each lung and spread into smaller passages.
  • Alveoli: Tiny air sacs where gas exchange occurs. [NCBI – Alveoli]
  • Lungs: Main respiratory organs responsible for taking in oxygen and expelling CO₂.
  • Diaphragm: A muscle that contracts and relaxes to allow breathing.

Function

The main function is to inhale oxygen and exhale carbon dioxide. This process is essential for cellular respiration and energy production in all body tissues. Learn more at Visible Body – Respiratory System.

Process of Breathing

  • Inhalation: The diaphragm contracts, chest expands, and air flows into lungs.
  • Gas Exchange: Oxygen moves into the blood; CO₂ moves into alveoli.
  • Exhalation: The diaphragm relaxes, pushing air with CO₂ out of the lungs.

Interesting Facts

  • The average adult breathes about 12–20 times per minute.
  • Lungs contain over 300 million alveoli.
  • The total surface area of lungs is about the size of a tennis court.

Diseases & Disorders

  • Asthma: A chronic condition causing airway inflammation and difficulty breathing.
  • Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD): A group of lung diseases including emphysema and chronic bronchitis. [CDC – COPD]
  • Pneumonia: Infection that inflames the alveoli in one or both lungs.
  • Lung Cancer: A serious disease often linked to smoking and environmental exposure.
