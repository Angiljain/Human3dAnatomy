Cardiovascular System

Explore how blood circulates throughout the body.

Overview

The cardiovascular system, also known as the circulatory system, is responsible for transporting blood, oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste products throughout the body. It is a closed-loop system composed of the heart, blood vessels, and blood.

Main Components

  • Heart: A muscular organ that pumps blood throughout the body. It has four chambers – two atria and two ventricles.
  • Arteries: Blood vessels that carry oxygen-rich blood away from the heart to tissues.
  • Veins: Blood vessels that return oxygen-poor blood back to the heart.
  • Capillaries: Tiny blood vessels where gas and nutrient exchange occurs between blood and tissues.

Functions

  • Transport of oxygen from lungs to tissues and carbon dioxide from tissues to lungs.
  • Distribution of nutrients from the digestive system to the body.
  • Hormone delivery from glands to target organs.
  • Waste removal via kidneys and liver.
  • Immune system support by transporting white blood cells.
  • Regulation of body temperature and fluid balance.

How It Works

The heart pumps blood in a double circulatory system. Oxygen-poor blood returns from the body to the right atrium, is pumped to the right ventricle, and then to the lungs where it receives oxygen. The oxygen-rich blood then returns to the left atrium, goes into the left ventricle, and is pumped out to the rest of the body through the aorta.

Common Cardiovascular Diseases

  • Hypertension: High blood pressure that can damage vessels and organs.
  • Atherosclerosis: Hardening of arteries due to plaque buildup.
  • Heart Attack: Blockage of blood flow to heart muscle.
  • Stroke: Interrupted blood supply to the brain.
  • Arrhythmias: Irregular heartbeat patterns.

Fun Facts

  • Your heart beats about 100,000 times per day.
  • The blood vessels in your body could circle the Earth more than twice (about 100,000 km).
  • The heart pumps about 1.5 gallons of blood every minute.
